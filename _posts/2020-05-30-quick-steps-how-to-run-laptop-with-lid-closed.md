<h2 id="1">Run Laptop with Lid Closed:</h2>
Most of us connect our laptops to external monitors for better productivity and experience. Once you connect the laptop to an external screen, you will keep your laptop lid open even you are working on the external monitor because closing the lap will turn off it.

By default, in Windows 10 and earlier versions, the laptop goes into the sleep mode when you close the laptop screen. It is possible to put the laptop keep running even after closing the lid without entering into the sleep or hibernate state. Here are the steps.
<h2 id="2">Steps to Keep your Laptop ON with Lid Closed:</h2>
<ol>
 	<li>Go to the <strong>Start</strong> menu. In the search box, you have to type <strong>'control panel'</strong> and hit <strong>Enter</strong>.

[caption id="attachment_297" align="alignnone" width="880"]<img class="size-full wp-image-297" src="https://windowsdot.com/wp-content/uploads/2020/05/nt3.png" alt="Control Panel" width="880" height="680" /> Control Panel[/caption]</li>
 	<li>
<blockquote>Alternatively, on Windows 10, you can head to <strong>Settings &gt; System &gt; Power &amp; sleep</strong> and select <strong>Additional power settings</strong> from the right menu or else click the <strong>Battery icon</strong> in the <strong>System Tray</strong> and choose <strong>Power Options</strong>.</blockquote>
</li>
 	<li>You have to click on the <strong>Hardware and Sound</strong> option.

[caption id="attachment_347" align="alignnone" width="813"]<img class="size-full wp-image-347" src="https://windowsdot.com/wp-content/uploads/2020/05/lc1.png" alt="Hardware and Sound" width="813" height="505" /> Hardware and Sound[/caption]</li>
 	<li>Continue to click on <strong>Power Options</strong>.

[caption id="attachment_348" align="alignnone" width="813"]<img class="size-full wp-image-348" src="https://windowsdot.com/wp-content/uploads/2020/05/lc2.png" alt="Power Options" width="813" height="505" /> Power Options[/caption]</li>
 	<li>In the left menu, you have to click on the <strong>Choose what closing the lid does </strong>option.

[caption id="attachment_349" align="alignnone" width="813"]<img class="size-full wp-image-349" src="https://windowsdot.com/wp-content/uploads/2020/05/lc3.png" alt="Choose what closed lid does" width="813" height="505" /> Choose what closed lid does[/caption]</li>
 	<li>You can find the <strong>When I close the lid</strong> section. The drop-down menu will show <strong>Sleep, Hibernate, Shut down, and Do nothing</strong> as per the laptop's power state <strong>'On battery'</strong> or <strong>'Plugged in'</strong>.</li>
 	<li>You have to select the <strong>'Do nothing'</strong> option.</li>
 	<li>Then, click on the <strong>Save changes</strong> button.

[caption id="attachment_350" align="alignnone" width="813"]<img class="size-full wp-image-350" src="https://windowsdot.com/wp-content/uploads/2020/05/lc4.png" alt="Choose Do Nothing option" width="813" height="505" /> Choose Do Nothing option[/caption]</li>
 	<li>Now, you must be able to close your laptop lid without it going into sleep mode.</li>
</ol>
<p id="note"><strong>Note: </strong>If you change the<strong> 'On battery'</strong> setting to <strong>'Do nothing'</strong> always make sure your laptop is Shut down or in either Sleep or Hibernation mode when you place it in your bag to prevent overheating.</p>
